Movie Review: Punching The Clown

Directed by: Gregori Viens

Written by: Gregori Viens, Henry Phillips

Starring: Audrey Siegel, Ellen Ratner, Henry Phillips, Matthew Walker

Genre: Comedy, Drama

MPAA Rating: Unrated

Punching The Clown is about comedic songwriter Henry Philips (played by Henry Philips) who comes to L.A. to try and jump start his career.  He winds up getting mistaken for someone else and signed to a record deal but just as everything is starting to go well, another miscommunication rips it all away.

I’d heard a lot about this movie over the past few years, including it’s famous glowing recommendation from Sarah Silverman and finally got a chance to see it.  I really didn’t know what to expect going in even after so much hype but what I got was a solid film that was understated in it’s comedy.

The plot itself is kind of hilarious but often involves just a lot of sadness.  The whole movie is filled with sadness, like Henry’s brother who plays a non-Batman character at childrens birthday parties.  But within this mood of slow and sad there’s some big laughs that aren’t super played up.  This isn’t a movie that says “laugh here”, it’s just funny.

It’s clearly low budget and has absolutely no polish to it.  It’s very raw and realistic.  I often will complain about the lack of production value but it kinda works for Punching The Clown.  It’s an example of low budget independent filmmaking that just works.  There should be more independent films like this.

I really recommend checking this out.  It’s going to take you a little bit to get with the tone but you’ll find yourself enjoying it once you do.
